1

From "Archivist" to "Bodyguard"!

(The Production Effect)

• Problem: Words you only read silently go to the brain's 'Archive' (slow retrieval).

• Solution: Speaking aloud tags the word with 'Your Own Voice,' making it a 'Precious Memory' that jumps to long-term recall.

Speaking words aloud improves memory retention by 14%-20% over silent reading. This is the "Production Effect."

Source: MacLeod et al., 2010. University of Waterloo Study
2

End the "Freezing"!

(Fluency & Prosody Improvement)

• Problem: You break the rhythm struggling with grammar and native speed.

• Solution: Shadowing is the 'Language Treadmill'! You follow the native audio in real-time, imprinting accent and rhythm (Prosody) directly into your muscle memory.

Shadowing is proven to be the most efficient technique for increasing pronunciation accuracy and oral fluency.

Source: Hamada & Suzuki, 2022. Related Research
3

"No More Overload!"

(Cognitive Load & Short Story)

• Problem: Long texts cause brain overload, leading to quick burnout and quitting.

• Solution: Short stories are the 'Optimal Data Units' your brain can process without crashing. You absorb the full context without fatigue.

Managing information load boosts 'Working Memory' efficiency and greatly improves learning success.

Source: Cognitive Load Theory
4

"Translation Off!"

(Contextual Processing)

• Problem: Your brain runs a constant 'Translation App' when you hear Korean.

• Solution: The "Sound + Short Story" combination skips the translation process. You absorb the full context and react immediately.

Production practice improves vocabulary and grammatical comprehension better than comprehension-focused study alone.

Source: Hopman & MacDonald, 2018. Wisconsin-Madison University
